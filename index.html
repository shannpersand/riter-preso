Introducing Riter:

A friendly slab-ish serif typeface created for average people doing average things. Riter is a year-long project conceptualized and vaguely executed at the 2016 Type@Cooper West program by Shannon Miwa. Have a look around at Riter, a typeface for mundane tasks.
  • Term One
  • Term Two
  • Term Three

Inspiration

Term One Inspiration
Initial inspiration for the uppercase came from the Trajan column.
For the lowercase, the Ramsey Psalter served as one inspiration.
Edward Johnston's foundational hand was also a reference.

Inspiration

In term two, I began to look more closely at typewriter faces, namely the IBM Selectric faces.
IBM Selectric faces: Advocate and Scribe for serif inspiration
IBM Selectric faces: Artisan and Letter Gothic for letter construction inspiration
I also began to pay more attention to Input from Font Bureau
...and to Operator by Hoefler and Co.

Inspiration

In term three, I started looking at different ways to expand the Riter family.
Starting with something bold, hoping to find the limit of the bold: black? ultra?
Then, testing the other side of the spectrum with the extreme light
Another parameter I tried was width, both narrow
...and wide
I also considered a high contrast version for 'reading'
